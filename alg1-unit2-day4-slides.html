Today's Objective

To apply addition, subtraction, and multiplication skills to solve a variety of polynomial problems.

Warm-Up: Operation Quick Check

Let's do a quick check on the three operations we've learned.

SUBTRACT: \((m^2 - m + 5) - (4m^2 + m - 2)\)

MULTIPLY: \((y + 8)(y - 3)\)

Strategy: Decode the Problem

Word problems require you to be a detective. Look for keywords that tell you which operation to use. Pay close attention to the order of operations (PEMDAS).

Addition

Sum, total, combine, perimeter, increase

Subtraction

Difference, how much more, remove, decrease

Multiplication

Product, area, volume, of

I Do: Multi-Step Area Problem

A square has a side length of \(3x+2\). A smaller square with side length \(x\) is removed. What is the area of the remaining shape?

Step 1 (Large Area): \(A_{L} = (3x+2)^2 = 9x^2 + 12x + 4\)

Step 2 (Small Area): \(A_{S} = (x)^2 = x^2\)

Step 3 (Subtract): \((9x^2 + 12x + 4) - (x^2)\)

\(8x^2 + 12x + 4\)

I Do: Mixed Operations

Subtract the product of \((x-1)\) and \((x+4)\) from \((5x^2 - 2x + 10)\).

Step 1: Handle parentheses first. Find the product.

\((x-1)(x+4) = x^2 + 4x - x - 4 = x^2 + 3x - 4\)

Step 2: Now, perform the subtraction. The word "from" means the second polynomial comes first!

\((5x^2 - 2x + 10) - (x^2 + 3x - 4)\)

Step 3: Distribute the negative and combine like terms.

\(5x^2 - 2x + 10 - x^2 - 3x + 4\)

\(4x^2 - 5x + 14\)
