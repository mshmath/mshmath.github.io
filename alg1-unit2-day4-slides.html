Unit 2: Polynomial Operations

Day 4: Factoring Trinomials (a=1)

Lesson Objective

To be able to factor trinomials of the form \(x^2 + bx + c\).

Warm-Up: From Multiplication to Factoring

Remember how we multiplied two binomials?

\((x+2)(x+5) = x^2 + 7x + 10\)

Today, we learn how to start with the trinomial and find the original factors!

The Key to Factoring Trinomials

To factor \(x^2 + bx + c\), we need to find two "magic numbers".

We are looking for two numbers that:

  1. MULTIPLY to the last term (c).
  2. ADD to the middle term's coefficient (b).

I Do: Finding the Magic Numbers

Let's factor \(x^2 + 7x + 10\).

We need two numbers that multiply to 10 and add to 7.

Factors of 10

1, 10

2, 5

Their Sum

\(1+10=11\)

\(2+5=7\)

The magic numbers are +2 and +5.

I Do: Writing the Factors

We found our numbers are +2 and +5. Now we just write the answer!

// Step 1: Start with two sets of parentheses with x at the front.

\((x \quad )(x \quad )\)

// Step 2: Place your two magic numbers inside.

\((x+2)(x+5)\)

Check your answer: Multiply \((x+2)(x+5)\) to see if you get back to \(x^2+7x+10\)!

We Do: Let's Try With Negatives

Factor this trinomial as a class: \(x^2 - 8x + 12\)

What two numbers...

...MULTIPLY to +12?

...ADD to -8?

Hint: If they multiply to a positive but add to a negative, both numbers must be negative.

You Do: On Your Own

Factor the following polynomial completely.

\(x^2 + 3x - 18\)

Hint: If they multiply to a negative, one number is positive and one is negative.

\((x+6)(x-3)\)
