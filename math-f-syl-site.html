Course Overview

Welcome to Math Foundations! This self-paced online course is designed to build the essential pre-algebra skills needed for success in Algebra 1.

My Role: While the lessons are online, I am here to guide you, provide one-on-one help, and lead mini-lessons when you need extra support.

Materials Needed

  • Notebook or binder for notes
  • Pencils and erasers
  • Calculator (Scientific or Desmos)
  • Laptop/Computer with internet

Class Routine & Flow

1. Start of Class Come in, grab your paper, pencil, and Chromebook. Be ready to work!
2. Warm Up Complete 1-2 problems on the board. We will discuss these together.
3. Main Lesson I use slides and examples on the board. Please take notes during this time.
4. Practice Work on a practice worksheet (Independent or Group work).
5. Closing Complete the Exit Ticket to show what you learned.

Submitting Work: All work must be turned into the tray or handed directly to me to be graded.

Make-Up Work & Support

Fridays are the best days for making up material, but we can talk and be flexible to accommodate your schedule as needed.

Accommodations: If you have specific needs (such as larger fonts, printed notes, or preferential seating), please let me know immediately so I can ensure you have the tools to succeed.

Units & Topics

Aligned with Virginia SOLs for Pre-Algebra.

  • Unit 1: Whole Number Foundations
    • Place value, estimation, operations (+, -, x, √∑), PEMDAS.
  • Unit 2: Understanding Fractions
    • Visualizing fractions, simplifying, comparing sizes.
  • Unit 3: Decimals & Percents
    • Connecting fractions, decimals, and percents ("out of 100").
  • Unit 4: Operations
    • Real-world math with fractions and decimals (money, measurement).
  • Unit 5: Ratios & Rates
    • Proportional reasoning, unit rates (cost per ounce).
  • Unit 6: Applications
    • Solving everyday problems: discounts, tax, tips.
  • Unit 7: Intro to Algebra
    • Integers (pos/neg numbers), variables, writing expressions.
  • Unit 8: Equations & Graphing
    • Solving one- and two-step equations, plotting points (x, y).

Grading & Progress

This is a Competency-Based course. There are no traditional letter grades assigned by me.

Success = Completion. You must finish all required modules, practice sets, and assessments in the online platform to pass the course.

Late Work Policy

I will accept assignments up to one week late; after that, it is considered missed.

Communication is key! If you know you are not going to attend class, please communicate with me. I can have materials ready for you at the beginning of class when you return or send them home with you.

Please be respectful of everyone's time and be considerate.

Expectations

  1. Work Consistently: Log in and make progress every week.
  2. Take Notes: Treat the online videos like a real lecture.
  3. Ask for Help: Do not spin your wheels. If you are stuck, ask me!
