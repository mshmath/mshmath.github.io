Course Overview

This course is specifically designed for adult learners completing their high school credit requirements. The curriculum aligns with the Virginia Standards of Learning (SOL) for Geometry, focusing on the properties of shapes, logical reasoning, and spatial relationships.

The objective is to build mathematical confidence and competence through clear instruction and consistent practice. Emphasis is placed on understanding core concepts rather than rote memorization.

Course Schedule

The following schedule outlines the progression of topics aligned with VA SOL standards. Dates are tentative and subject to adjustment based on class pacing.

Week Dates Topic & Standards
Week 1 Jan 6 - 9 Reasoning, Logic & Lines
Conditional statements, deductive reasoning, and transversal lines. (SOL G.1, G.2)
*Class begins Tuesday, Jan 6
Week 2 Jan 13 - 16 Coordinate Geometry
Distance formula, midpoint formula, and slope of parallel/perpendicular lines. (SOL G.3)
Week 3 Jan 20 - 23 Transformations & Symmetry
Translations, reflections, rotations, and symmetry. (SOL G.3, G.4)
Week 4 Jan 27 - 30 Triangle Properties
Triangle congruence postulates and logical proofs. (SOL G.5, G.6)
Week 5 Feb 3 - 6 Right Triangles & Trigonometry
Pythagorean Theorem and trigonometric ratios (Sine, Cosine, Tangent). (SOL G.8)
Week 6 Feb 10 - 13 Polygons & Circles
Properties of quadrilaterals, interior/exterior angles, and circle arcs. (SOL G.9, G.11)
Week 7 Feb 17 - 20 Three-Dimensional Figures
Calculating surface area and volume of 3D solids. (SOL G.13, G.14)
Week 8 Feb 24 - 27 Review & Final Examination
Comprehensive review of all SOL standards followed by the final exam.

Course Policies & Accommodations

Professional Conduct: A respectful and focused environment is required for everyone's success. Please limit personal device usage to breaks.

Attendance & Communication: Regular attendance is critical for an 8-week accelerated course. Please communicate any absences via email promptly. Assignments are generally available digitally for make-up work.

Accessibility: This course aims to be accessible to all learners. If you require specific accommodations (e.g., printed materials in larger font, specific seating, or extended time on assessments), please discuss this with the instructor privately to ensure your needs are met.
