Course Overview

Welcome to Geometry! This course is specifically designed for adult learners (ages 21+) who are completing their high school credit requirement. This course is 8 weeks in length.


Each class will begin with a warm-up or topic overview to introduce the day’s concepts, followed by guided notes and example problems we complete together. Students will then have dedicated time to work independently or in small groups on problems for practice.


Our focus is on building understanding through clear explanations, gaining math confidence, hands-on problem-solving, and targeted practice to prepare for success and application in the real world.

Units & Topics Covered

All units and topics in this course are directly aligned with the Virginia Standards of Learning (SOL) for Geometry, ensuring a comprehensive and relevant curriculum.


  • Unit 1: Reasoning, Logic & Venn Diagrams: Understanding logical reasoning and its application in geometry.
  • Unit 2: Lines, Angles, and Parallelism: Properties of lines, angles, and parallel lines, including transversal relationships.
  • Unit 3: Transformations & Symmetry: Exploring translations, reflections, rotations, dilations, and various types of symmetry.
  • Unit 4: Triangles: Properties & Congruence: Classifying triangles, understanding properties of angles and sides, and proving congruence.
  • Unit 5: Similarity & Trigonometry: Concepts of similar figures and an introduction to right-triangle trigonometry.
  • Unit 6: Polygons & Circles: Properties of various polygons, circumference, area, and equations of circles.
  • Unit 7: 3D Figures: Volume, Surface Area, and Dimensional Change: Calculating volume and surface area of three-dimensional figures and analyzing effects of changing dimensions.

Classroom Expectations

To foster a positive and productive learning environment, all students are expected to:

  1. Be respectful: To yourself, your peers, and the learning environment.
  2. Come prepared: Bring all required materials and be ready to engage with the day's lesson.
  3. Follow directions: For all activities, assignments, and assessments.
  4. Stay engaged: Participate actively in lessons and dedicated practice time.
  5. Use technology appropriately: Laptops and other devices should only be used for class-related work.
  6. Try your best: Don't be afraid to ask for help and utilize the support systems available to you.

Late Work and Makeup Policy

  • Late Work: Late assignments will be accepted within one week of the original due date for partial credit (up to 90%).
  • Absences: If you are absent, it is your responsibility to check with the instructor (via email or in person) for any missed notes, assignments, or materials.
  • Make-up Assessments: Make-up assignments, tests, and quizzes must be scheduled with the instructor as soon as you return to class.
