Algebraic Expressions

Day 2: Evaluate with Substitution

Today's Objectives

  • Substitute given values for variables.
  • Apply the Order of Operations to evaluate expressions.
  • Evaluate expressions with absolute value and square roots.

Warm-Up: Order of Operations

Simplify each expression:

  1. 5 + 2 × 10
  2. (8 - 3)²
  3. 16 ÷ 4 + 2

The 2-Step Process: Plug and Chug!

Step 1: Substitute (Plug)

Replace the variable with its given value. Use parentheses!

Step 2: Simplify (Chug)

Use the Order of Operations (PEMDAS) to find the final value.

Example 1: I Do

Evaluate 3x + 5 when x = 4

1. Substitute: 3(4) + 5

2. Multiply: 12 + 5

3. Add: 17

Example 2: We Do

Evaluate k² + 4m when k = 5 and m = -2

1. Substitute: (5)² + 4(-2)

2. Exponent: 25 + 4(-2)

3. Multiply: 25 - 8

4. Subtract: 17

Independent Practice

Choose your level and evaluate each expression.

RED

  1. Evaluate `x + 10` when `x = 7`.
  2. Evaluate `4y` when `y = 6`.
  3. Evaluate `z - 3` when `z = 15`.

YELLOW

  1. Evaluate `2a + 3b` when `a = 5` and `b = 2`.
  2. Evaluate `c² - 1` when `c = 8`.
  3. Evaluate `(d + 4) / 2` when `d = 10`.

GREEN

  1. Evaluate `|m - n|` when `m = 5` and `n = 12`.
  2. Evaluate `√p + q²` when `p = 36` and `q = -3`.
  3. Cost for friends is `12f + 5f`. Find the cost for 4 friends.
