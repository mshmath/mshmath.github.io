Let's Understand the Tiles: Why the Shapes?

The name of each tile comes from its area. We define the side lengths of the shapes first, and that determines what the tile represents.

If a large square has a side length of x...


x x

...its Area is \(x \cdot x = x^2\).

So, this is the "x-squared" tile.

If a rectangle has sides x and 1...


1 x

...its Area is \(x \cdot 1 = x\).

So, this is the "x" tile.

If a small square has a side length of 1...


1 1

...its Area is \(1 \cdot 1 = 1\).

So, this is the "one" tile.

The Power of "Zero Pairs"

The entire system works because of a simple idea: opposites cancel. Think of it like \(+5\) and \(-5\) making 0. A positive tile and its matching negative tile form a zero pair and can be removed.

We Do: Applying the Concepts

How can we use polynomial operations to solve problems about shapes?

1. The perimeter of a triangle is \(7x-10\). If two sides are \(x+5\) and \(3x-2\), what expression represents the third side?

Hint: Start by adding the two sides you know. Then, subtract that sum from the total perimeter.

2. Find the area of a rectangle with length \(2x+1\) and width \(x+3\).

Hint: Remember, the formula for the area of a rectangle is Length × Width. You will need to multiply the two binomials.
