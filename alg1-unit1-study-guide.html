Unit 1 Study Guide

Algebraic Expressions & Evaluation

Day 1: Translating Expressions & Order of Operations

1. Write an algebraic expression for "the sum of a number and 8".

Let 'x' represent the number. The word "sum" indicates addition. So, the expression is $x + 8$.

2. Write an algebraic expression for "the product of 6 and a number, decreased by 2".

Let 'x' be the number. "Product" means multiplication (6x), and "decreased by" means subtraction. So, the expression is $6x - 2$.

3. Evaluate using the order of operations: $20 - 4 \div 2 + 3 \cdot 5$

PEMDAS: First, division and multiplication from left to right: $20 - 2 + 15$. Then, addition and subtraction from left to right: $18 + 15 = 33$.

Day 2: Evaluating Expressions

4. Evaluate $5a - 2b$ when $a = 4$ and $b = 3$.

Substitute the values into the expression: $5(4) - 2(3) = 20 - 6 = 14$.

5. Evaluate $x^2 + 3y$ when $x=5$ and $y=2$.

Substitute the values: $(5)^2 + 3(2) = 25 + 6 = 31$.

Day 3: Simplifying with Properties of Exponents

6. Simplify: $y^3 \cdot y^7$

Product Rule: When multiplying powers with the same base, you add the exponents: $y^{3+7} = y^{10}$.

7. Simplify: $(2a^4)^3$

Power Rule: Distribute the exponent to each factor inside the parentheses: $2^3 \cdot (a^4)^3 = 8a^{12}$.

8. Simplify: $\frac{x^9}{x^3}$

Quotient Rule: When dividing powers with the same base, you subtract the exponents: $x^{9-3} = x^6$.

Day 4: Combining Like Terms

9. Simplify: $10x - 4y + 3x + 6y$

Combine the x-terms: $10x + 3x = 13x$. Combine the y-terms: $-4y + 6y = 2y$. The simplified expression is $13x + 2y$.

Day 5: Application & Review

10. A triangle has sides of length $x$, $2x+1$, and $3x-4$. What is the expression for its perimeter?

Perimeter is the sum of all sides. Add the like terms: $(x + 2x + 3x) + (1 - 4) = 6x - 3$.
