Worked Example

Solve the system: $y = x + 3$ and $2x + 3y = 4$.

  1. Step 1 (Identify): Notice the first equation is already solved for $y$. This is our substitution piece.
  2. Step 2 (Substitute): Plug the expression $(x+3)$ into the second equation in place of $y$.
    $2x + 3(x+3) = 4$.
  3. Step 3 (Solve for $x$): Distribute and solve the new equation.
    $2x + 3x + 9 = 4$
    $5x + 9 = 4$
    $5x = -5$
    $x = -1$
  4. Step 4 (Solve for $y$): Plug $x=-1$ back into the easy equation: $y = (-1) + 3 = 2$.
  5. Step 5 (Solution): The solution is the point $(-1, 2)$.
