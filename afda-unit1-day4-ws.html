Worked Example

Describe all transformations for the equation $f(x) = 3(x-1)^2 + 4$

  1. Dilation: The '3' in front causes a vertical stretch (the graph will be skinnier).
  2. Horizontal Shift: The $(x-1)$ inside moves the graph right 1 unit.
  3. Vertical Shift: The $+4$ at the end moves the graph up 4 units.
