I Do: Solving by Elimination

Elimination means we add the two equations together to make one of the variables disappear. This works best when the variables are lined up and one pair are opposites (like $+3y$ and $-3y$).

$x+y=5$
$2x-y=1$

  1. Step 1: Notice that $+y$ and $-y$ are opposites. This is perfect for elimination!
  2. Step 2: Add the two equations together, straight down in columns.
    $(x+2x) + (y-y) = (5+1)$
    $3x + 0 = 6 \Rightarrow 3x=6$
  3. Step 3: Solve this new, simple equation for $x$.
    $x = 2$
  4. Step 4: Plug $x=2$ back into the first (easy) equation to find $y$.
    $(2) + y = 5 \Rightarrow y=3$
  5. Step 5: The solution is the point $(2, 3)$.

We Do: When There Are No Opposites

Sometimes you need to multiply one equation to create opposites.

$x+2y=7$
$3x-y=0$

  1. The $y$ terms are close to being opposites ($+2y$ and $-y$). What can we multiply the second equation by to make them cancel? (Answer: 2)
  2. Our new system is:
    $x+2y=7$
    $6x-2y=0$
  3. Now add them together. What do you get?
    $7x = 7 \Rightarrow x=1$
  4. Plug $x=1$ back in to find $y$.
    $3(1)-y=0 \Rightarrow 3=y$
  5. What is the final solution? $(1, 3)$
