Day 1: Solving Systems by Graphing

Objective: To find the solution of a system of two linear equations by graphing them and finding their point of intersection.

Notes

A system of equations is just two or more equations that we work with at the same time. For linear equations, the graphs are straight lines.

The solution to the system is the one point $(x, y)$ where the lines cross or intersect. This point is the only point that makes both equations true.

There are three possibilities:

  • The lines cross at one point (one solution).
  • The lines are parallel and never cross (no solution).
  • The lines are the exact same line (infinitely many solutions).

Desmos Exploration: Finding the Intersection
  1. In Desmos, type your first equation: y = 2x - 3.
  2. In a new line, type your second equation: y = -x + 3.
  3. Look at the graph. The two lines cross at a single point. Click on that point!
  4. Desmos will show you a gray dot with the coordinates of the intersection. In this case, it's $(2, 1)$. That's the solution to the system.

Day 2: Solving Systems by Substitution

Objective: To solve a system of equations algebraically using the substitution method.

Notes

Substitution means to replace one thing with something it is equal to. This method is best when one of the equations is already solved for a variable (like $y = \dots$ or $x = \dots$).

Steps for Substitution:
  1. Solve one equation for one variable (e.g., get $y$ by itself).
  2. Substitute this expression into the *other* equation.
  3. Solve the new, single-variable equation.
  4. Plug your answer back into one of the original equations to find the second variable.
Step-by-Step Example

Solve the system: $y = x + 1$ and $3x + 2y = 8$.

  1. Step 1: The first equation is already solved for $y$. It tells us that $y$ is the same as $x+1$.
  2. Step 2: In the second equation, replace the $y$ with $(x+1)$. This gives us: $3x + 2(x+1) = 8$.
  3. Step 3: Solve this new equation for $x$.
    $3x + 2x + 2 = 8$ (Distribute)
    $5x + 2 = 8$ (Combine like terms)
    $5x = 6$ (Subtract 2)
    $x = 6/5$ or $1.2$.
  4. Step 4: Plug $x=1.2$ back into the easy equation: $y = (1.2) + 1 = 2.2$.
  5. Solution: The solution is the point $(1.2, 2.2)$. You can check this by graphing in Desmos!

Day 3: Solving Systems by Elimination

Objective: To solve a system of equations algebraically by adding or subtracting the equations to eliminate a variable.

Notes

Elimination is a method for solving systems where you add the two equations together to make one of the variables disappear. This method is best when both equations are in standard form ($Ax + By = C$) and have variables that can be easily cancelled out.

The goal is to get one variable to have the same coefficient but the opposite sign in each equation (like $+2y$ and $-2y$).

Step-by-Step Example

Solve the system: $2x + 3y = 4$ and $x - 3y = -1$.

  1. Step 1: Look at the equations. Notice that we have a $+3y$ in the first equation and a $-3y$ in the second. They are perfect opposites!
  2. Step 2: Add the two equations together, column by column.
    $(2x + x) + (3y - 3y) = (4 - 1)$
    $3x + 0 = 3$
    $3x = 3$.
  3. Step 3: Solve the resulting equation for $x$: $x = 1$.
  4. Step 4: Plug $x=1$ back into either original equation to find $y$. Let's use the second one: $(1) - 3y = -1 \Rightarrow -3y = -2 \Rightarrow y = 2/3$.
  5. Solution: The solution is $(1, 2/3)$.

Day 4: Systems of Linear Inequalities

Objective: To represent the solution to a system of two linear inequalities graphically.

Notes

This combines what we learned about systems and inequalities. The goal is to graph two or more inequalities on the same plane.

The solution is the area on the graph where all the shaded regions from all the inequalities overlap. Any point in this overlapping region is a solution.

Remember the rules for graphing a single inequality:

  • Solid Line for $\le$ or $\ge$.
  • Dotted Line for $<$ or $>$.
  • Shade above for $y > \dots$ and below for $y < \dots$.

Desmos Exploration: Finding the Overlap
  1. In Desmos, graph the first inequality, for example: y < 2x + 1.
  2. In a new line, graph the second inequality, for example: y >= -x - 2.
  3. Desmos shades each inequality with a different color.
  4. The solution to the system is the section of the graph where the two colors overlap.
