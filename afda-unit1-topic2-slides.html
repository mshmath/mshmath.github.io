The "Parent" Concept

Think of the Parent Function (like $y=x^2$) as the "Original Character".

We can add numbers to the equation to move the character around, but the basic shape stays the same.

Rigid Transformation: Moving without changing shape (Shifting/Flipping/Rotation).

The Master Formula

Every transformation fits into this pattern:

$y = \color{#dc2626}{a} \cdot f(x - \color{#2563eb}{h}) + \color{#16a34a}{k}$
a: Shape

Reflection (Negative)

Stretch ($|a| > 1$)

Compress ($0 < |a| < 1$)

h: Horizontal

Inside the function.

Moves Left or Right.

⚠️ Opposite Sign!
k: Vertical

Outside the function.

Moves Up or Down.

✅ Exact Sign

Vertical Shifts ($k$)

The Number on the OUTSIDE.
  • + k → UP
  • - k → DOWN

Think: "Like an elevator."

$y = x^2 \mathbf{\color{#16a34a}{+ 3}}$

Horizontal Shifts ($h$)

The Number on the INSIDE.
"Inside is a Liar!"
  • $(x - h)$ → RIGHT
  • $(x + h)$ → LEFT

$y = (x \mathbf{\color{#2563eb}{- 4}})^2$

Reflections

A reflection flips the graph over an axis using a Negative Sign.

X-Axis Reflection

Negative is OUTSIDE

$y = - f(x)$

(Upside Down)

Y-Axis Reflection

Negative is INSIDE

$y = f(-x)$

(Left-Right Swap)

Reflection Over X-Axis

Negative on the FRONT.

This flips all positive $y$-values to negative.

$y = \mathbf{\color{#dc2626}{-}}x^2$

Reflection Over Y-Axis

Negative on the INSIDE.

This flips the graph horizontally (Left $\leftrightarrow$ Right).

$y = \sqrt{\mathbf{\color{#9333ea}{-}}x}$

Rotation Basics

A Rotation turns a graph around a fixed point (The Origin).

Directions:
  • CCW: Counter-Clockwise (Standard Math Direction)
  • CW: Clockwise

We focus on 90°, 180°, and 270° rotations.

90° and 270° Rotations

90° CCW / 270° CW

Swap $(x, y)$ and make the first coordinate negative.

$(x, y) \to (-y, x)$
270° CCW / 90° CW

Swap $(x, y)$ and make the second coordinate negative.

$(x, y) \to (y, -x)$

180° Rotation (Origin)

Double Reflection.

Rotating 180° is the same as reflecting over BOTH the X and Y axes.

$(x, y) \to (-x, -y)$

Equation: $y = -f(-x)$

360° Rotation

$(x, y) \to (x, y)$

Full Circle!

You end up exactly where you started.

This is called the Identity Transformation.

Dilations ($a$)

The number multiplying the front changes the shape.

Vertical Stretch

Big Number ($|a| > 1$)

Gets Tall & Skinny

$y = \mathbf{3}x^2$

Vertical Compression

Fraction ($0 < |a| < 1$)

Gets Short & Wide

$y = \mathbf{\frac{1}{2}}x^2$

Transformation Summary

Negative Front Outside: $-f(x)$ Reflect X-Axis (Flip Vertical)
Negative Inside Inside: $f(-x)$ Reflect Y-Axis (Flip Horizontal)
Rotate 180° Both: $-f(-x)$ Origin Rotation (Double Neg)
Rotate 90° CCW Coord: $(-y, x)$ Swap and Negate First
a (Number) Front: $3f(x)$ Stretch ($>1$) or Compress ($<1$)
h (Horizontal) Inside: $(x-h)$ Left ($+$) or Right ($-$) [Opposite]
k (Vertical) Outside: $+k$ Up ($+$) or Down ($-$) [Exact]
