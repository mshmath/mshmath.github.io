Worked Example

Describe all transformations for the equation $f(x) = - (x+2)^2 + 3$

  1. Identify the Parent Function: Because of the $x^2$, the parent function is Quadratic.
  2. Horizontal Shift: The $(x+2)$ is inside the parentheses. This means a shift left 2 units (opposite of the sign).
  3. Reflection: The negative sign in front is outside the function, which means a reflection (flip) over the x-axis.
  4. Vertical Shift: The $+3$ at the end is outside the function, which means a shift up 3 units.
