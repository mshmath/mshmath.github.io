Substitute
To replace a variable (letter) with a number.
If x = 3, then 2x becomes 2(3).
Evaluate
To calculate the final numerical value of an expression.
2(3) + 1 = 7
The Golden Rule of Substitution:
ALWAYS put parentheses ( ) around the number you substitute in, especially if it is negative!
Correct: (-3)² = 9  |  Wrong: -3² = -9
Order of Operations (PEMDAS)
1. Parentheses ( )
Evaluate parts inside grouping symbols first. Includes brackets [ ] and fraction bars.
2. Exponents x²
Calculate powers and square roots.
5² = 25 | √16 = 4
3. Mult / Div × ÷
Perform multiplication and division from Left to Right. Do whichever comes first!
4. Add / Sub + −
Perform addition and subtraction from Left to Right. Do whichever comes first!
Special Symbols
Absolute Value | x |
Distance from zero. Makes the result positive.
| -5 | = 5    | 5 | = 5
Square Roots √x
What number times itself equals x?
√49 = 7 (because 7×7=49)
