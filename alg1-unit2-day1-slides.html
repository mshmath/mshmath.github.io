Day 1: Adding & Subtracting Polynomials

Today's Objective

To determine sums and differences of polynomial expressions in one variable.

What is a Polynomial?

Polynomial: A mathematical expression with variables, coefficients, and non-negative integer exponents.

Examples:

\(3x^2+2x-5\)

\(x^4y^2+3\)

Non-Examples:

\(n^{-3}+9\)

(Negative Exponent)

\(5m^n-8\)

(Variable Exponent)

Naming Polynomials by Terms

Monomial: A polynomial with one term. (e.g., \(7\) or \(6x\))

Binomial: A polynomial with two terms. (e.g., \(3t-1\))

Trinomial: A polynomial with three terms. (e.g., \(2x^2+3x-7\))

The Core Concept: Combining Like Terms

To add or subtract polynomials, we use a method called combining like terms. This is the most important skill for this lesson!

Like Terms Are...

Terms that have the same variable raised to the same power.

Example: \(3x^2\) and \(-8x^2\) are like terms. \(5x\) and \(5x^3\) are NOT.

I Do: Adding Polynomials

Let's find the sum of \((3x^2 + 5x - 4) + (2x^2 - 7x + 1)\).

Step 1: Group like terms together.

\((3x^2 + 2x^2) + (5x - 7x) + (-4 + 1)\)

Step 2: Combine the coefficients of the like terms.

\(5x^2 - 2x - 3\)

I Do: Subtracting Polynomials

Let's find the difference of \((8x^2 - 2x + 9) - (5x^2 + 3x - 1)\).

Step 1: Distribute the negative to the second polynomial. This is critical!

\(8x^2 - 2x + 9 \textcolor{red}{- 5x^2 - 3x + 1}\)

Step 2: Group and combine like terms.

\((8x^2 - 5x^2) + (-2x - 3x) + (9 + 1)\)

\(3x^2 - 5x + 10\)

We Do: Let's Try Together

More Vocabulary: Standard Form

Standard Form: Writing terms in order from highest to lowest exponent.

Example: \( \textcolor{violet}{7a^3} \textcolor{gray}{-2a^2} \textcolor{violet}{+8a} \textcolor{gray}{-1} \)

Degree of a Polynomial: The largest exponent of any single term.

Example: The degree of the polynomial above is 3.

Leading Coefficient: The coefficient of the term with the highest degree.

Example: The leading coefficient above is 7.

Another Method: The Vertical Way

You can also line up like terms vertically. It can help keep your work organized!

Addition

\(2g^2 + 6g - 4\)

+ \( g^2 - g \)

\(3g^2 + 5g - 4\)

Subtraction

Change to addition, and add the opposite!

\(4x^2 + 0x + 5\)

+ \(2x^2 - 4x + 7\)

\(6x^2 - 4x + 12\)
