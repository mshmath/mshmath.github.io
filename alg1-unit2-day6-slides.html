Unit 2: Polynomial Operations

Day 6: Dividing Polynomials

Lesson Objective

To be able to determine the quotient of polynomials by dividing by a monomial or a binomial.

Warm-Up: Exponent Rules!

Remember the Quotient Rule for exponents? When you divide, you subtract the exponents.

\(\frac{x^5}{x^2} = x^{5-2} = x^3\)

Part 1: Dividing by a Monomial

When dividing by a monomial (one term), you can split the problem into separate fractions.

\(\frac{6x^3 + 12x^2}{3x}\)

becomes

\(\frac{6x^3}{3x} + \frac{12x^2}{3x}\)

I Do: Dividing by a Monomial

Let's solve \(\frac{6x^3 + 12x^2}{3x}\).

Step 1: Split into separate fractions.

\(\frac{6x^3}{3x} + \frac{12x^2}{3x}\)

Step 2: Simplify each fraction. Divide the coefficients, subtract the exponents.

\(( \frac{6}{3}x^{3-1} ) + ( \frac{12}{3}x^{2-1} )\)

\(2x^2 + 4x\)

Part 2: Dividing by a Binomial

When dividing by a binomial (like \(x+2\)), we need a different method: Polynomial Long Division.

This process is very similar to the long division you learned in elementary school!

\( (x^2 + 7x + 10) \div (x+2) \)

is set up like this:

\( x+2 \overline{) x^2 + 7x + 10} \)

I Do: Polynomial Long Division

Let's solve \((x^2 + 7x + 10) \div (x+2)\).

Steps: Divide, Multiply, Subtract, Bring Down

          x   + 5       
        ____________
    x+2 | x² + 7x + 10
        -(x² + 2x)      (x²/x = x. Then x(x+2))
        ----------
             5x + 10    (Subtract, bring down 10)
            -(5x + 10)   (5x/x = 5. Then 5(x+2))
            ---------
                  0     (Subtract. Remainder is 0)

Answer: \(x+5\)
