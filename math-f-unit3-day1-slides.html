That little dot is a decimal point. It's a separator!

What is a Decimal?

A decimal is simply another way to write a fraction.

Decimals represent the "parts" or "pieces" of a whole number.

The Star of the Show: The Decimal Point

Its only job is to separate the whole numbers from the fractional parts.

Decimal Place Value

The places to the right of the decimal all end in "-ths".

Hundreds Tens Ones . Tenths Hundredths
100 10 1 \(\frac{1}{10}\) \(\frac{1}{100}\)

Visualizing Tenths

The first place after the decimal means we split one whole into 10 equal parts.

\(\frac{3}{10}\) = 0.3

"three tenths"

Visualizing Hundredths

The second place means we split one whole into 100 equal parts.

\(\frac{42}{100}\) = 0.42

"forty-two hundredths"

I Do: Reading and Writing Decimals

How do we read and write 1.25?

1.25

1. Read the whole number: "One"

2. Say "and" for the point.

3. Read the rest: "twenty-five"

4. Say the last place value: "hundredths"

"One and twenty-five hundredths"

How to Read and Write Decimals

  1. Read the whole number part.
  2. Say "" for the decimal point.
  3. Read the number to the right of the decimal.
  4. Say the place value of the digit.
