1. Linear Models

Constant Rate of Change
  • • Adds or subtracts the same amount each time.
  • • Straight Line graph.
Keywords:
"Per", "Every", "Constant rate", "Flat fee + hourly"

$y = mx + b$

Example: Pay \$15 per hour.

2. Quadratic Models

Symmetry & Gravity
  • • Goes up then down (or down then up).
  • • Has a maximum or minimum (Vertex).
  • • U-Shape (Parabola).
Keywords:
"Projectile", "Thrown", "Dropped", "Max Height", "Area"

$y = ax^2 + bx + c$

Example: A rocket launch path.

3. Exponential Models

Rapid Growth/Decay
  • • Multiplies by the same factor (Ratio).
  • • Starts slow, then shoots up (or drops fast then levels off).
  • • J-Curve.
Keywords:
"Percent growth", "Doubles", "Half-life", "Population", "Compound interest"

$y = a(b)^x$

Example: Bacteria doubling.

4. Logarithmic Models

Fast Start, Slow Finish
  • • Inverse of Exponential.
  • • Grows very fast at first, then levels off (plateau).
  • • Has a Vertical Asymptote.
Keywords:
"Decibels (Sound)", "Richter Scale (Quakes)", "pH level", "Plateau"

$y = \log(x)$

Example: Loudness of sound.

Model Summary

Linear Constant Change ($+$) Straight Line "Per", "Rate"
Quadratic Max/Min Peak U-Shape (Parabola) "Projectile", "Area"
Exponential Percent Growth ($\times$) J-Curve "Doubles", "Growth %"
Logarithmic Fast then Slow r-Shape "Decibels", "Scale"
