UNIT 1 • TOPIC 2

Evaluating Expressions

Substitution & Order of Operations

Standard A.EO.1.b

Objectives & Standards

Today's Goals:

  • Substitute given values for variables using parentheses.
  • Apply the Order of Operations (PEMDAS) to find the value of an expression.
  • Evaluate expressions involving Absolute Value and Square Roots.

Key Vocabulary

Substitute

To replace a variable with a number.

If x = 5,
then 3x becomes 3(5)
Evaluate

To find the numerical value (solve it).

3(5) + 1
15 + 1 = 16

The 2-Step Process

Step 1: Plug It In

Rewrite the expression, replacing every letter with its number.

ALWAYS put parentheses ( ) around the number you plug in!

Step 2: Chug It Out

Use PEMDAS to calculate the final answer.

Parentheses → Exponents → Mult/Div → Add/Sub

Why Parentheses Matter

Evaluate x² when x = -3

Correct Way (With Parentheses)

(-3)²

(-3) × (-3) = 9

Negative × Negative = Positive

Wrong Way (No Parentheses)

-3²

-(3 × 3) = -9

Calculator reads this as "Negative of 3 squared"

Guided Practice

Evaluate when a = 4 and b = -2

1. 3a + 5
3(4) + 5 = 12 + 5 = 17
2. 6b - 2
6(-2) - 2 = -12 - 2 = -14

Guided Practice: Exponents

Evaluate when x = 3 and y = -4

1. x² + 5
(3)² + 5 = 9 + 5 = 14
2. y² - 10
(-4)² - 10 = 16 - 10 = 6

Guided Practice: Special Symbols

Evaluate when m = 25 and n = -8

1. √m + 3
√25 + 3 = 5 + 3 = 8
2. | n | + 2
| -8 | + 2 = 8 + 2 = 10
