2. Evaluating & Order of Operations (SOL A.1)

PEMDAS: Parentheses $\rightarrow$ Exponents $\rightarrow$ Multiply/Divide $\rightarrow$ Add/Subtract.

Example: Evaluate $3x^2 - 5$ when $x = -2$
  • 1. Substitute: $3(-2)^2 - 5$ (Always use parentheses!)
  • 2. Exponents: $(-2)^2 = 4 \rightarrow 3(4) - 5$
  • 3. Multiply: $3(4) = 12 \rightarrow 12 - 5$
  • 4. Subtract: $7$
3. Laws of Exponents (SOL A.2)
Rule Name Formula Example
Product Rule $x^a \cdot x^b = x^{a+b}$ $x^2 \cdot x^3 = x^5$
Quotient Rule $\frac{x^a}{x^b} = x^{a-b}$ $\frac{y^8}{y^2} = y^6$
Power Rule $(x^a)^b = x^{a \cdot b}$ $(x^2)^3 = x^6$
Negative Exp. $x^{-a} = \frac{1}{x^a}$ $x^{-2} = \frac{1}{x^2}$
Zero Exp. $x^0 = 1$ $5^0 = 1$
Key Vocabulary
Expression Math phrase with numbers & variables. No equal sign.
Variable A letter representing an unknown number.
Coefficient The number multiplied in front of a variable.
Constant A number by itself with no variable.
Base The big number being multiplied in an exponent term.
Exponent The small number showing how many times to multiply.
