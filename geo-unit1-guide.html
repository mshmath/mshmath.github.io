1. Conditional Statements

If p, then q.

Conditional$p \rightarrow q$
Converse$q \rightarrow p$ (Flip)
Inverse$\sim p \rightarrow \sim q$ (Negate)
Contrapositive$\sim q \rightarrow \sim p$ (Both)
*Conditional & Contrapositive are Equivalent!
2. Biconditionals
Symbol: $p \leftrightarrow q$
Phrase: "if and only if" (iff)
Rule: Both the Conditional ($p \rightarrow q$) AND Converse ($q \rightarrow p$) must be TRUE.
3. Logic Laws
  • Law of Detachment:
    If $p \rightarrow q$ is true, and $p$ happens, then $q$ happens.
  • Law of Syllogism:
    If $p \rightarrow q$ and $q \rightarrow r$, then $p \rightarrow r$. (The Chain Rule).
  • Counterexample:
    One example proving a statement False.
4. Venn Diagrams
  • Union ($\cup$): "OR" (Everything shaded).
  • Intersection ($\cap$): "AND" (Middle overlap).
  • Complement ($\sim$): "NOT" (Outside circle).
  • Subset: "All A are B" (Circle inside circle).
