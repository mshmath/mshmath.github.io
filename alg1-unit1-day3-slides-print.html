Laws of Exponents

Day 3: Product, Quotient, and Power Rules

Slide 2

Today's Objectives

  • Apply the Product Rule when multiplying powers.
  • Apply the Quotient Rule when dividing powers.
  • Apply the Power Rule when raising a power to a power.
Slide 3

Warm-Up: Discover the Pattern

Write in expanded form and simplify:

1. $x^3 \cdot x^2 = (x \cdot x \cdot x) \cdot (x \cdot x) = ?$

2. $y^5 / y^3 = (y \cdot y \cdot y \cdot y \cdot y) / (y \cdot y \cdot y) = ?$

Slide 4

Product Rule

When multiplying powers with the same base, ADD the exponents.

$a^m \cdot a^n = a^{m+n}$

Example: $(2y^4)(3y^2) = 6y^{4+2} = 6y^6$

Slide 5

Quotient Rule

When dividing powers with the same base, SUBTRACT the exponents.

$a^m / a^n = a^{m-n}$

Example: $10b^8 / 2b^3 = 5b^{8-3} = 5b^5$

Slide 6

Power Rule

When raising a power to another power, MULTIPLY the exponents.

$(a^m)^n = a^{m \cdot n}$

Example: $(3k^4)^2 = 3^2k^{4 \cdot 2} = 9k^8$
