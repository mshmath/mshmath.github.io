Worked Example

Solve the system: $x+2y=7$ and $3x-y=0$.

  1. Step 1 (Identify): Notice no variables are opposites. However, if we multiply the second equation by $2$, the $y$ terms will become $+2y$ and $-2y$.
  2. Step 2 (Multiply): Multiply the entire second equation by $2$: $2(3x-y=0)$ becomes $6x-2y=0$.
  3. Step 3 (Eliminate): Add the first equation and the new second equation:
    $(x+2y=7)$
    $+ (6x-2y=0)$
    ------------------
    $7x = 7$
  4. Step 4 (Solve for $x$): Solve the new equation: $7x=7 \Rightarrow x=1$.
  5. Step 5 (Solve for $y$): Plug $x=1$ back into one of the original equations: $3(1)-y=0 \Rightarrow 3=y$.
  6. Step 6 (Solution): The solution is the point $(1, 3)$.
