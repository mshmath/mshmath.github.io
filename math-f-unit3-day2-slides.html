Day 2: Comparing & Ordering

Which is a better deal?

Store A

$1.50

vs.

Store B

$1.05

To find out, we need to compare the decimals!

Today's Objectives

  • Learn two strategies to compare decimals.
  • Practice putting decimals in order from least to greatest.
  • Use the symbols <, >, = correctly.

Strategy 1: Line 'Em Up!

The best way to compare decimals is to use a place value chart.

Compare 0.7 and 0.55

Ones . Tenths Hundredths
0 . 7
0 . 5 5

Since 7 > 5 in the tenths place, 0.7 > 0.55.

Strategy 2: Annex Zeros

"Annex" just means "to add". You can add zeros to the end of a decimal without changing its value.

This helps make the decimals the same length!

0.7

\(\rightarrow\)

0.70

Now it's easy to see that 0.70 (seventy hundredths) is greater than 0.55 (fifty-five hundredths).

Ordering Decimals

To put several decimals in order, use the same strategies!

Order from least to greatest: 0.8, 0.45, 1.2

0.45

The correct order is: 0.45, 0.8, 1.2

I Do: Compare and Order

Compare

Which is greater, 2.45 or 2.4?

1. Line them up: 2.45 and 2.4

2. Annex a zero: 2.45 and 2.40

3. Compare: 45 is bigger than 40.

2.45 > 2.4

Order

Least to greatest: 0.8, 0.45, 1.2

1. Annex zeros: 0.80, 0.45, 1.20

2. Compare the numbers: 80, 45, 120.

3. Smallest is 45, then 80, then 120.

0.45, 0.8, 1.2

Strategy 1: Line 'Em Up!

The best way to compare decimals is to use a place value chart. This helps you compare digits in the same place value, starting from the left.

Example: Compare 0.7 and 0.55

Ones . Tenths Hundredths
0 . 7
0 . 5 5

Since 7 > 5 in the tenths place, we know that 0.7 > 0.55.

Strategy 2: Annex Zeros

You can add zeros to the end of a decimal without changing its value. This makes both decimals the same length, which makes them easier to compare.

Example:

0.7 becomes 0.70

Now it's easy to see that seventy hundredths (0.70) is greater than fifty-five hundredths (0.55).
