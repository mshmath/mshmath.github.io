Day 1: Translating Words into Math

Objective: To translate verbal phrases into algebraic expressions and understand key vocabulary.

Notes

An algebraic expression is a mathematical phrase containing numbers, variables, and operations. It does not have an equal sign.

A Variable is a letter, like $x,$ used to represent an unknown .
A Coefficient is the number being multiplied by a .

Keywords tell us which operation to use.
"Sum," "more than," and "increased by" all mean addition.
"Difference," "less than," and "decreased by" mean subtraction.
"Product" and "of" mean multiplication.
"Quotient" means division.

Watch out for "turnaround" words like than and from. They reverse the order of the terms. For example, "10 less than a number" is written as $x - 10,$ not $10 - x.$

Examples

I Do: Multi-Step Translation

  1. First, identify the main operation. "Product" means we are multiplying.
  2. What are we multiplying? We are multiplying "4" and "the sum of a number and 2."
  3. "The sum of a number and 2" needs to be grouped together. We write this as $(n+2).$
  4. Now, combine the parts: $4 \cdot (n+2),$ which simplifies to $4(n+2).$

Day 2: Evaluating Expressions

Objective: To evaluate algebraic expressions for given variable values using the order of operations.

Notes

To evaluate an expression, we first substitute the given values for each variable. This means replacing the letters with their numbers. It is extremely important to use parentheses when you substitute, especially with negative numbers.

After substituting, we simplify using the Order of Operations to make sure everyone gets the same answer. We use the acronym GEMDAS:

  • Grouping Symbols: Includes ( ), [ ], | |, and fraction bars. Always work from the inside out.
  • Exponents: Calculate any powers.
  • Multiplication and Division: Work from left to right, whichever comes first.
  • Addition and Subtraction: Work from left to right, whichever comes first.

Examples

I Do: Evaluating with Negatives

Evaluate $5a^2 - (b + 2c)$ when $a=-2, b=6, c=-1.$

  1. Substitute with parentheses: $5(-2)^2 - (6 + 2(-1))$
  2. Grouping Symbols: Inside the parentheses, multiply first: $2(-1) = -2.$ Now it's $5(-2)^2 - (6 - 2).$ Finish the group: $6-2=4.$ The expression is now $5(-2)^2 - 4.$
  3. Exponents: Calculate $(-2)^2 = (-2)(-2) = 4.$ The expression becomes $5(4) - 4.$
  4. Multiplication: $5(4) = 20$. Now we have $20 - 4.$
  5. Subtraction: $20 - 4 = 16.$ The final answer is 16.

Day 3: Working with Exponents

Objective: To simplify expressions using the laws of exponents (product, quotient, power, zero, negative).

Notes

An exponent is a shortcut for repeated multiplication. In $x^a,$ $x$ is the base and $a$ is the exponent.

  • Product Rule: When multiplying terms with the same base, ADD the exponents. $x^2 \cdot x^3 = (x \cdot x) \cdot (x \cdot x \cdot x) = x^5.$
  • Quotient Rule: When dividing terms with the same base, SUBTRACT the exponents. $\frac{x^5}{x^2} = \frac{x \cdot x \cdot x \cdot x \cdot x}{x \cdot x} = x^3.$
  • Power of a Power Rule: When raising a power to another power, MULTIPLY the exponents. $(x^2)^3 = x^2 \cdot x^2 \cdot x^2 = x^6$.
  • Zero Exponent Rule: Anything to the power of zero is 1. For example, $\frac{x^3}{x^3} = x^{3-3} = x^0$, but we also know $\frac{x^3}{x^3}=1$. So, $x^0=1.$
  • Negative Exponent Rule: A negative exponent means "take the reciprocal". To make it positive, move the term across the fraction bar. $x^{-2} = \frac{1}{x^2}.$

Examples

I Do: Combining Multiple Rules

Simplify: $\frac{(2x^4y^2)^3}{4x^5y^7}$

  1. Power Rule first (on the numerator): Distribute the exponent 3 to everything inside: $2^3 \cdot (x^4)^3 \cdot (y^2)^3$. This simplifies to $8x^{12}y^6.$
  2. Rewrite the expression: Now we have $\frac{8x^{12}y^6}{4x^5y^7}.$
  3. Quotient Rule: Divide the coefficients $8/4=2.$
    Subtract the exponents for x $12-5=7.$
    Subtract the exponents for y $6-7=-1.$
  4. Combine the results: We get $2x^7y^{-1}.$
  5. Negative Exponent Rule: The $y^{-1}$ is unhappy on top, so it moves to the bottom to become $y^1.$ The final answer is $\frac{2x^7}{y}.$

Day 4: Simplifying Expressions

Objective: To simplify algebraic expressions by using the distributive property and combining like terms.

Notes

Like Terms are terms that have the exact same variables raised to the exact same exponents. The coefficients can be different. To combine like terms, you add or subtract the coefficients and keep the variable part the same.

The Distributive Property allows us to multiply a term into a set of parentheses. You multiply the outside term by every single term on the inside. $a(b+c) = ab + ac$. Be very careful when distributing a negative sign!

To simplify an expression, the process is: 1. Distribute to remove any parentheses. 2. Combine any like terms.

Examples

I Do: Distribute and Combine

Simplify: $3(x^2 - 2x + 5) - (4x^2 - x - 6)$

  1. Distribute the 3: $3 \cdot x^2 = 3x^2,$ $3 \cdot (-2x) = -6x,$ $3 \cdot 5 = 15.$ First part is $3x^2 - 6x + 15.$
  2. Distribute the negative: Think of the minus sign as a -1. $-1 \cdot 4x^2 = -4x^2,$ $-1 \cdot (-x) = +x,$ $-1 \cdot (-6) = +6.$ Second part is $-4x^2 + x + 6.$
  3. Rewrite the full expression: $3x^2 - 6x + 15 - 4x^2 + x + 6.$
  4. Combine Like Terms: Group the terms. $(3x^2 - 4x^2) + (-6x + x) + (15 + 6).$
  5. Simplify each group: $-x^2 - 5x + 21.$ This is the final answer.
