Slide 4

Zero Exponent Rule

Anything divided by itself is 1. Let's see what that means for exponents:

$x^3 / x^3 = x^{3-3} = x^0$. Also, $x^3 / x^3 = 1$.

So, $a^0 = 1$ (for any non-zero a)

Slide 5

Negative Exponent Rule

A negative exponent means "move it across the fraction bar" to make it positive.

$a^{-n} = 1/a^n$

Example: $x^{-3} = 1/x^3$

Slide 6

Putting It All Together

Simplify: $(10a^5b^3) / (2a^2b^7)$

1. Group like terms: $(10/2) \cdot (a^5/a^2) \cdot (b^3/b^7)$

2. Quotient Rule: $5 \cdot a^{5-2} \cdot b^{3-7} = 5a^3b^{-4}$

3. Negative Exponent Rule: $5a^3 / b^4$
