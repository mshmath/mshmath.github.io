What is Probability?

  • Probability: A number from 0 to 1 that shows how likely something is to happen (how good its chance is).
  • 0 = Impossible (it will never happen).
  • 0.5 (or 12) = Equally Likely (it has a 50/50 chance).
  • 1 = Certain (it will always happen).

Two Types of Probability

Theoretical Probability

This is what should happen based on math. (It's the "perfect world" guess).

Example: A coin should land on heads 12 of the time.

Experimental Probability

This is what actually happens when you do an experiment. (It's what happens in the "real world" when you test it).

Example: You flip a coin 10 times and it actually lands on heads 4 times.

Theoretical Probability

The Formula:

P(event)= # of Favorable Outcomes Total # of Possible Outcomes

Favorable outcomes: This is what you want to happen (like rolling a 4). It's the "winner" or "target" you're looking for.

Total possible outcomes: This is all the things that could happen (like rolling 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, or 6). It's every single possibility.

I Do: What is P(rolling a 4) on a 6-sided die?

Step 1: Find the "Favorable" number.
How many faces on the die are a "4"? Just one.
So, our favorable number is 1.

Step 2: Find the "Total" number.
How many faces are on the die in total? Six (1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6).
So, our total number is 6.

Step 3: Write the fraction.
We write the probability as (Favorable / Total).
The answer is 16.

Experimental Probability

The Formula:

P(event)= # of times event happened Total # of trials

# of times event happened: How many times your event (like "Heads") actually showed up in your experiment.

Total # of trials: The total number of times you tried the experiment (how many times you flipped the coin, spun the spinner, etc. in total).

I Do: I tossed a coin 20 times. It landed on HEADS 12 times. What is P(Heads)?

Step 1: Find the "Happened" number.
How many times did "Heads" happen? The problem says 12 times. This is our top number.

Step 2: Find the "Total" number.
How many times did we flip the coin in total? The problem says 20 times. This is our bottom number.

Step 3: Write the fraction.
We write the probability as (Happened / Total).
The answer is 1220.

You Do: Independent Practice

Green Level

Yellow Level

Red Level

A spinner has 4 equal sections (A, B, C, D). You spin it 40 times. It lands on A 12 times. Why is the *experimental* probability different from the *theoretical* probability?
