Unit 2: Polynomial Operations

Day 2: Multiplying Polynomials

Today's Objective

To determine the product of polynomial expressions using the distributive property and area models.

The Core Concept

To multiply polynomials, you must multiply every term in the first polynomial by every term in the second. No term gets left behind!

Method 1: Distributive Property

Pass out each term from the first polynomial to every term in the second.

Method 2: Area Model (Box)

A visual way to organize all the multiplications so you don't miss any.

I Do: The Distributive Property

Let's find the product of \((x + 4)(2x - 3)\).

Step 1: Distribute the \(x\) and the \(+4\).

\(\textcolor{blue}{x}(2x - 3) \textcolor{red}{+ 4}(2x - 3)\)

Step 2: Multiply.

\(\textcolor{blue}{2x^2 - 3x} \textcolor{red}{+ 8x - 12}\)

Step 3: Combine like terms.

\(2x^2 + 5x - 12\)

I Do: The Area Model

Let's find the product of \((x + 4)(2x - 3)\) again.

Step 1: Set up the box. Write one polynomial on top, the other on the side.

\(2x\)\(-3\)
\(x\)
\(+4\)

Step 2: Multiply to fill each box. Then combine like terms.

\(2x^2 - 3x + 8x - 12 \rightarrow 2x^2 + 5x - 12\)

Multiplying Larger Polynomials

The Area Model is very useful when multiplying bigger polynomials, like a binomial and a trinomial.

\((x - 6)(x^2 + 2x + 1)\)

\(x^2\)\(+2x\)\(+1\)
\(x\)\(x^3\)\(2x^2\)\(x\)
\(-6\)\(-6x^2\)\(-12x\)\(-6\)

Combine like terms: \(x^3 + (2x^2 - 6x^2) + (x - 12x) - 6\)

\(x^3 - 4x^2 - 11x - 6\)

Special Products (Shortcuts!)

Squaring a Binomial: \((a+b)^2 = a^2 + 2ab + b^2\)

\( (x+7)^2 = x^2 + 2(x)(7) + 7^2 = x^2+14x+49 \)

Difference of Squares: \((a+b)(a-b) = a^2 - b^2\)

\( (2b+5)(2b-5) = (2b)^2 - 5^2 = 4b^2 - 25 \)
