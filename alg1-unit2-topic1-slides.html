Classifying by Terms

Count the parts separated by $+$ or $-$ signs.

Example: $3x^2$

Example: $x + 5$

Example: $x^2 + 2x + 1$
Polynomial "Poly" = Many. A general name for expressions with many terms.

Adding Polynomials

The Method
  1. Drop the parentheses (they don't matter for addition).
  2. Combine Like Terms (Same letter, Same exponent).

Think: "Apples with Apples, Oranges with Oranges"

$(2x + 5) + (3x - 2)$

$2x + 3x = \mathbf{5x}$

$5 - 2 = \mathbf{3}$

$5x + 3$

Guided Practice: Adding

1. $(3x + 4) + (2x + 5)$
$5x + 9$
2. $(x^2 - 3) + (2x^2 + 7)$
$3x^2 + 4$

Subtracting Polynomials

Strategy: Distribute the Negative

That minus sign belongs to EVERYTHING in the second group.

When you see a minus sign outside parenthesis:

$-(x - 3)$

It changes every sign inside.

$+x$ becomes $-x$
$-3$ becomes $+3$

$(4x + 7) \mathbf{- (x - 3)}$

↓ Distribute ↓

$4x + 7 \mathbf{- x + 3}$

$3x + 10$
