Equations give us one point as a solution. Inequalities give us a whole area!

I Do: Graphing One Inequality

The solution to an inequality is a whole shaded area. Here are the rules:

$y < 2x+1$

Line Type: Use a Dotted Line for $<$ or $>$. The line is not part of the solution.

Line Type: Use a Solid Line for $\le$ or $\ge$. The line is included in the solution.

Shading: Shade ABOVE the line for $y > \dots$

Shading: Shade BELOW the line for $y < \dots$

We Do: Finding the Solution Area

The solution to a system of inequalities is the area where all the shading overlaps. This is called the "feasible region".

$y \ge x - 1$
$y < -2x + 5$

  1. Step 1: Go to Desmos.com.
  2. Step 2: Type in the first inequality, $y \ge x-1$. Notice the solid line and shading.
  3. Step 3: Type in the second inequality, $y < -2x+5$. Notice the dotted line and shading.
  4. Step 4: The solution is the darker area where the two shaded regions overlap. Any point in this area works for both inequalities!
