Algebra 1 Posters

Real Numbers

The set of all rational and irrational numbers.

Rational Numbers

Numbers that can be written as a ratio of two integers (e.g., $2\frac{3}{5}, -5, 0.3, \sqrt{16}$)

Integers

$\{... -3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3 ...\}$

Whole Numbers

$\{0, 1, 2, 3, 4 ...\}$

Natural Numbers

$\{1, 2, 3, 4 ...\}$

Irrational Numbers

Non-repeating, non-terminating decimals (e.g., $\sqrt{7}, \pi$)

Absolute Value

The distance between a number and zero on a number line. Distance is always non-negative.

Order of Operations

  1. 1.
    Grouping Symbols First, evaluate expressions within \( (\ ), [ \ ], \{ \}, | \ |, \) and \( \sqrt{} \).
  2. 2.
    Exponents Next, evaluate all terms with exponents (e.g., \(a^n\)).
  3. 3.
    Multiplication & Division Then, perform these from left to right.
  4. 4.
    Addition & Subtraction Finally, perform these from left to right.

Expression

A representation of a quantity that may contain numbers, variables, or operation symbols. It does not have an equals sign.

Examples

\(x\) \(-\sqrt{26}\) \(3^4 + 2m\) \(ax^2 + bx + c\) \(3(y + 3.9)^2 - \frac{8}{9}\)

Variable

A symbol, usually a letter, that represents an unknown value or a quantity that can change.

Examples

In \( \textcolor{#ef4444}{A} = \pi \textcolor{#f97316}{r}^2 \), the variables are A and r.

In \( \textcolor{#ef4444}{d} = 7\textcolor{#f97316}{c} - 5 \), the variables are d and c.

Coefficient

The numerical factor that is multiplied by a variable in a term.

Examples

In \(-4 + \textcolor{#ef4444}{2}\textcolor{#f97316}{x}\), the coefficient is \(2\).

In \(\textcolor{#ef4444}{\frac{2}{3}}\textcolor{#f97316}{ab} - \frac{1}{2}\), the coefficient is \(\frac{2}{3}\).

In \(\textcolor{#ef4444}{\pi}\textcolor{#f97316}{r^2}\), the coefficient is \(\pi\).

Term

A single number, a variable, or numbers and variables multiplied together. Terms are separated by \(+\) or \(-\) signs.

\(\textcolor{#f97316}{3x} \textcolor{#ef4444}{+} \textcolor{#3b82f6}{2y} \textcolor{#ef4444}{-} \textcolor{#0d9488}{8}\) has 3 terms.

\(\textcolor{#f97316}{-5x^2} \textcolor{#ef4444}{-} \textcolor{#3b82f6}{x}\) has 2 terms.

\(\textcolor{#f97316}{\frac{2}{3}ab}\) is 1 term.

Exponential Form

A concise way of writing repeated multiplication of the same number.

\( \textcolor{#f97316}{a}^{\textcolor{#3b82f6}{n}} \)

base, exponent

Examples

\(2 \cdot 2 \cdot 2 = \textcolor{#f97316}{2}^{\textcolor{#3b82f6}{3}} = 8\)

\(n \cdot n \cdot n \cdot n = \textcolor{#f97316}{n}^{\textcolor{#3b82f6}{4}}\)

\(3 \cdot 3 \cdot 3 \cdot x \cdot x = \textcolor{#f97316}{3}^{\textcolor{#3b82f6}{3}}\textcolor{#f97316}{x}^{\textcolor{#3b82f6}{2}} = 27x^2\)

Negative Exponent

A non-zero base raised to a negative exponent is equal to the reciprocal of the base raised to the positive exponent.

Property

\[ \textcolor{#f97316}{a}^{\textcolor{#ef4444}{-n}} = \frac{1}{\textcolor{#f97316}{a}^{\textcolor{#3b82f6}{n}}}, \quad \text{for } a \neq 0 \]

Examples

\[ 4^{\textcolor{#ef4444}{-2}} = \frac{1}{4^{\textcolor{#3b82f6}{2}}} = \frac{1}{16} \]

Zero Exponent

Any non-zero number raised to the power of zero is equal to 1.

Property

\[ \textcolor{#f97316}{a}^{\textcolor{#3b82f6}{0}} = 1, \quad \text{for } a \neq 0 \]

Examples

\[ (-5)^{\textcolor{#3b82f6}{0}} = 1 \] \[ 4m^{\textcolor{#3b82f6}{0}} = 4 \cdot 1 = 4 \]

Product of Powers

To multiply two powers with the same base, you add their exponents.

Property

\[ \textcolor{#f97316}{a}^{\textcolor{#3b82f6}{m}} \cdot \textcolor{#f97316}{a}^{\textcolor{#ef4444}{n}} = \textcolor{#f97316}{a}^{\textcolor{#3b82f6}{m} \textcolor{#0d9488}{+} \textcolor{#ef4444}{n}} \]

Examples

\[ x^{\textcolor{#3b82f6}{4}} \cdot x^{\textcolor{#ef4444}{2}} = x^{\textcolor{#3b82f6}{4}\textcolor{#0d9488}{+}\textcolor{#ef4444}{2}} = x^6 \]

Power of a Power

To raise a power to another power, you multiply the exponents.

Property

\[ (\textcolor{#f97316}{a}^{\textcolor{#3b82f6}{m}})^{\textcolor{#ef4444}{n}} = \textcolor{#f97316}{a}^{\textcolor{#3b82f6}{m} \textcolor{#0d9488}{\cdot} \textcolor{#ef4444}{n}} \]

Examples

\[ (y^{\textcolor{#3b82f6}{4}})^{\textcolor{#ef4444}{2}} = y^{\textcolor{#3b82f6}{4} \textcolor{#0d9488}{\cdot} \textcolor{#ef4444}{2}} = y^8 \]

Power of a Product

To find the power of a product, find the power of each factor and then multiply.

Property

\[ (\textcolor{#f97316}{a}\textcolor{#ef4444}{b})^{\textcolor{#3b82f6}{m}} = \textcolor{#f97316}{a}^{\textcolor{#3b82f6}{m}} \textcolor{#ef4444}{b}^{\textcolor{#3b82f6}{m}} \]

Examples

\[ (-3\textcolor{#f97316}{a}^4\textcolor{#ef4444}{b})^{\textcolor{#3b82f6}{2}} = (-3)^{\textcolor{#3b82f6}{2}} \cdot (\textcolor{#f97316}{a}^4)^{\textcolor{#3b82f6}{2}} \cdot \textcolor{#ef4444}{b}^{\textcolor{#3b82f6}{2}} = 9a^8b^2 \]

Quotient of Powers

To divide two powers with the same base, you subtract the exponents.

Property

\[ \frac{\textcolor{#f97316}{a}^{\textcolor{#3b82f6}{m}}}{\textcolor{#f97316}{a}^{\textcolor{#ef4444}{n}}} = \textcolor{#f97316}{a}^{\textcolor{#3b82f6}{m} \textcolor{#0d9488}{-} \textcolor{#ef4444}{n}}, \quad \text{for } a \neq 0 \]

Examples

\[ \frac{x^{\textcolor{#3b82f6}{6}}}{x^{\textcolor{#ef4444}{5}}} = x^{\textcolor{#3b82f6}{6}\textcolor{#0d9488}{-}\textcolor{#ef4444}{5}} = x^1 = x \]

Power of a Quotient

To find the power of a quotient, find the power of the numerator and the denominator, then divide.

Property

\[ \left(\frac{\textcolor{#f97316}{a}}{\textcolor{#ef4444}{b}}\right)^{\textcolor{#3b82f6}{m}} = \frac{\textcolor{#f97316}{a}^{\textcolor{#3b82f6}{m}}}{\textcolor{#ef4444}{b}^{\textcolor{#3b82f6}{m}}}, \quad \text{for } b \neq 0 \]

Examples

\[ \left(\frac{y}{3}\right)^{\textcolor{#3b82f6}{4}} = \frac{y^{\textcolor{#3b82f6}{4}}}{3^{\textcolor{#3b82f6}{4}}} = \frac{y^4}{81} \]
